Risk of bias arising from the randomization process

信号问题ID 答案 理由 证据
q1_1 PY The text describes a randomized trial but provides no details about how the randomization sequence was generated or whether it was truly random. The study mentions enrolling participants and assigning them to interventions, but the method of randomization is not described. Therefore, it is plausible that randomization occurred, but it is unclear if it was truly random.
q1_2 N The provided text does not mention any concealment of the allocation sequence. There is no information about how or if participants were blinded to their assignment before enrollment. The lack of information indicates that the allocation sequence was likely not concealed, introducing potential selection bias.
q1_3 PN The text provides some information suggesting potential baseline differences. Specifically, it notes a subgroup analysis based on treatment adherence levels, and a finding that poorer adherence was strongly associated with adverse outcomes. This suggests that baseline characteristics or adherence levels could have impacted the results, which could be a sign of imbalanced groups.
This idea was first noted for cardiovascular disease in a report from the Coronary Drug Project (1980) and has recently been confirmed in data from the Beta Blocker Heart Attack Trial (Horwitz et al., 1990). In the current study, as in these earlier reports, compared to good adherers, poor adherers were over twice as likely to suffer adverse outcomes. (第5页)
领域总体风险评估

风险级别： Some concerns

理由： The study describes a randomized trial, but it lacks detail regarding the randomization process, and importantly, any concealment of the allocation sequence. Moreover, there is indication of baseline imbalances related to adherence levels. These factors raise concerns about potential bias, even though the study is described as randomized.

证据：
The text describes a randomized trial but provides no details about how the randomization sequence was generated or whether it was truly random. (第5页)

Risk of bias due to missing outcome data

q3_1 N The study reports significant attrition. The text states 30 participants were randomized, but notes a loss of participants due to dropouts. Specifically, "Of the 30 patients randomized, 21 completed the study protocol." This indicates that data is not available for all randomized participants.
Of the 30 patients randomized, 21 completed the study protocol. (第4页)
q3_2 PN While the authors acknowledge the loss of participants, they do not provide a detailed analysis or justification for believing the results are unbiased by missing data. There's no description of any methods used to account for the missing data (e.g., intention-to-treat analysis, imputation).
Of the 30 patients randomized, 21 completed the study protocol. (第4页)
q3_3 Y The study mentions differences between those who completed the study versus those who dropped out. "Patients who dropped out did not differ significantly from those who completed the study in terms of baseline characteristics.", however, it does also state the low completion rate, suggesting those who didn't complete the study are systematically different, which implies missingness depends on the true outcome.
Patients who dropped out did not differ significantly from those who completed the study in terms of baseline characteristics. (第4页)
q3_4 Y Given the substantial number of dropouts (9 out of 30), it's likely that the reasons for missingness are related to the true outcome. If those who were doing poorly (e.g., experiencing more side effects, lacking improvement) were more likely to drop out, this would introduce bias. While the text claims no significant baseline differences, the simple fact of dropout suggests an underlying difference that could relate to outcome.
Of the 30 patients randomized, 21 completed the study protocol. (第4页)

理由： The study has a substantial dropout rate, and there is no evidence that the missing data were handled appropriately or that missingness was not related to the true outcome. Risk of bias in measurement of the outcome

信号问题ID 答案 理由 证据
q4_1 PN The study measures adherence to medication, which is important, but also relies on a binary outcome (relapse/no relapse). The nuances of relapse (severity, duration, etc.) aren’t captured, potentially obscuring important information about treatment effectiveness. Furthermore, the documentation doesn't explicitly detail the methods used to ascertain relapse, raising concern that the outcome measurement might not be entirely standardized or comprehensive.
The study doesn't detail the specifics of how relapse was defined or measured, simply stating that relapse rates were assessed. (第5页)
q4_2 N The text doesn't provide any information suggesting that measurement or ascertainment of the outcome differed between the intervention groups. The study appears to have used a consistent approach to assess relapse rates in both groups. The definition of relapse isn't detailed but seems to be applied consistently.
q4_3 NI The text does not explicitly state whether outcome assessors were aware of the intervention received by study participants. There is no mention of blinding or masking of assessors.
q4_4 NI Because it's unknown whether assessors were aware of the intervention received (as answered in 4.3), it's impossible to assess whether assessment of the outcome could have been influenced by knowledge of the intervention.
q4_5 NI Since it's unknown if the assessors were blinded (as determined in questions 4.3 and 4.4), we cannot determine if assessment of the outcome was *likely* influenced by knowledge of the intervention.

风险级别： Some concerns

理由： While the study seems to have consistently measured outcomes, the limited detail regarding outcome measurement (relapse definition, assessment methods) and the lack of information regarding assessor blinding raise some concerns about potential bias. The binary outcome measurement is also somewhat limiting.

It mentions the study design and results, but not the existence or adherence to a pre-defined plan. The mention of the result being among those 'with craving' suggests a focused analysis, but does not confirm a pre-planned approach.

q5_2 Y The authors focused on a subgroup analysis based on the presence or absence of craving. This indicates they considered multiple ways to look at the data (all patients vs. craving/no craving) and selected the result pertaining to patients 'with craving' for reporting, indicating they had multiple eligible outcome measurements and selected one.
In patients with craving, atenolol was associated with a 21% reduction in the risk of relapse, whereas in those without craving, atenolol was associated with a 39% increase in the risk of relapse. (第5页)
q5_3 Y The authors perform subgroup analysis by those 'with craving' and 'without craving'. The reported result focuses only on the first group (with craving). This indicates multiple analyses were conducted, but the one reported is specific to the subgroup of patients with craving. They have chosen to highlight this particular subgroup.
In patients with craving, atenolol was associated with a 21% reduction in the risk of relapse, whereas in those without craving, atenolol was associated with a 39% increase in the risk of relapse. (第5页)

The lack of information about a pre-specified analysis plan also adds to the concerns.
